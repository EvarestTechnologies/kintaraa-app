# What is Kintaraa?

Kintaraa is a mobile application that connects GBV survivors to a coordinated network of service providers — including healthcare workers, legal professionals, law enforcement, counselors, social services, GBV rescue centers, and community health workers — through a single, role-aware interface.

## The core function

A survivor opens the app. They report an incident — anonymously if they choose. The platform routes their case to the appropriate providers based on urgency, provider type, availability, and location. Providers receive the assignment, accept or decline, and coordinate care directly through the app.

## Who uses it

Kintaraa serves two primary groups:

**Survivors** — individuals who have experienced gender-based violence and need access to services. The app gives them a way to report incidents, request specific types of help, track their case, communicate with providers, and access safety planning tools — all from their phone.

**Service providers** — professionals who respond to GBV cases. Seven specializations are supported, each with a tailored dashboard:

| Provider Type | Primary Function |
|---|---|
| Healthcare | Patient management, medical records, appointments |
| Legal | Case management, court dates, document handling |
| Law Enforcement | Evidence tracking, case reports, investigations |
| Counseling/Therapy | Session notes, client tracking, therapy resources |
| Social Services | Resource coordination, benefit assistance |
| GBV Rescue Centers | Crisis intervention, hotline management, emergency response |
| Community Health Workers (CHW) | Field outreach, location-based services, referrals |

## What makes it distinct

Most GBV response systems are either paper-based or rely on disconnected tools (phone calls, spreadsheets, WhatsApp groups). Kintaraa provides:

- **Coordinated case routing** — cases go to the right providers automatically based on urgency and type
- **Role-specific interfaces** — each provider sees only what is relevant to their work, reducing cognitive load and data exposure
- **Offline capability** — survivors can submit reports without an internet connection; data syncs when connectivity is restored
- **Anonymous reporting** — survivors are not required to create an account or disclose their identity
- **Biometric security** — Face ID and fingerprint authentication for quick, secure access on mobile devices

## What it is not

Kintaraa is not an emergency dispatch service (though it connects survivors to emergency providers). It is not a messaging platform, a standalone counseling tool, or a replacement for the judgment of trained professionals. It is coordination infrastructure — the connective tissue between a survivor in crisis and the services that can help.
